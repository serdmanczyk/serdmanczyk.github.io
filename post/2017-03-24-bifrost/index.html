Bifröst - a queryable in-process worker queue (golang)

March 24, 2017

When you give a mouse a cookie, he writes a job queue

I was working on Freyr, and decided I wanted to implement the ability to post a list of readings. The first things that pop to mind when implementing such a handler are that you want to guard against the following:

  • Requests that are too large (default in go net/http)
  • DDOS attacks, mitigable by rate limiting (external library)
  • Prevent the user from waiting on multiple database writes

I could of course have just gotten away with accepting a list of readings, posting them to the database in order (or as one INSERT statement), and called it a day. But implementing a method of dispatching work into the background was just too enticing.

Why not goroutines?

I also could have just dispatch a goroutine doing the database insert, returned a 204, and called it a day. But how would the user get status?

There’s also the risk of launching too many goroutines and running out of memory. Not likely in my project, but another I didn’t want to sit in.

Using a job queue and a set number of workers, you rate the amount of work that can execute at a given time.
